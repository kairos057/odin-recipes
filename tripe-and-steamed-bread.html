Tripe and Steamed Bread

Description

This is a traditional South African dish that combines tender tripe with soft steamed bread. It's a hearty meal that is both comforting and flavorful.

Ingredients

Mogodu and Dombolo Ingredients

Tripe

  • 1 kg Cleaned beef or mutton tripe (mogodu), cut into pieces
  • 1 large Onion, chopped
  • 2 medium Potatoes, peeled and cubed (to thicken the gravy)
  • 1 Beef stock cube (like Knorr)
  • 1 teaspoon Robertsons Curry Powder (optional, for mild warmth)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Water for boiling

Steamed Bread

  • 3 cups Cake flour
  • 1 packet (10g) Instant dry yeast
  • 2 tablespoons Sugar
  • 1 teaspoon Salt
  • 1 and a half cups Warm water
  • 2 tablespoons Melted butter or oil (optional, for softness)

Cooking Instructions

Tripe

  1. Place the thoroughly cleaned tripe pieces into a large pot and cover completely with water.
  2. Bring to a boil, then turn down the heat to low. Cover with a lid and simmer slowly for 2 to 3 hours until the meat is tender. (Add water as needed to keep it covered).
  3. Add the chopped onion, cubed potatoes, beef stock cube, and curry powder.
  4. Simmer for another 20 to 30 minutes until the potatoes are soft and the gravy has thickened. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Steamed Bread

  1. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the cake flour, instant dry yeast, sugar, and salt.
  2. Gradually add the warm water (and melted butter if using) and mix until a soft dough forms.
  3. Knead the dough on a lightly floured surface for about 5 to 10 minutes until it is smooth and elastic.
  4. Place the dough back in a greased bowl, cover it with a warm cloth, and let it rise for 45 minutes until doubled in size.
  5. Punch down the dough. You can either place the whole dough ball directly on top of your simmering stew, or place it in a greased heat-proof bowl inside a large pot of boiling water.
  6. Cover tightly with a lid and steam undisturbed for 30 to 40 minutes without opening the pot. (Test with a toothpick—it should come out clean).
